Academic Standards (K-12)

Standards, Assessments and Graduation Requirements

The Minnesota K–12 Academic Standards are the statewide expectations for student learning in K–12 public schools. The standards are supplemented by benchmarks that identify the knowledge and skills that all students must achieve in a content area by the end of a grade level or grade band. School districts are required to put state standards into place so all students have access to high-quality content and instruction.

Per Minnesota Statutes 2025, section 120B.018, an academic standard is a summary description of student learning in a content area. Academic standards are comprised of one or more benchmarks. Benchmarks are the specific knowledge or skill that a student must master to complete part of an academic standard by the end of a grade level or grade band. The following resource may be helpful to use with parents: 

  • Understanding Minnesota K–12 Academic Standards and Parent Questions

Content areas are reviewed and revised based on a schedule in Minnesota Statutes 2025, section 20B.021, subd.4 (Revisions and Reviews Required). Minnesota’s academic standards are reviewed and revised on a 10-year cycle. The following resources provide more information on the timelines and process: 

  • Minnesota K–12 Academic Standards Development Process
  • Standards Implementation Table

Assessment

Schools and districts determine how their students will learn the Minnesota K-12 Academic Standards for each content area by developing courses, curriculum, and instructional materials aligned to the academic standards. When formative assessments are aligned to standards-based learning goals and embedded throughout instruction, teachers can identify where students are at in their learning based on evidence, to guide them where to go next. Summative evidence of learning is usually collected at the end of a grading period. Both types of classroom assessment can help identify student misconceptions and scaffold future instruction. Learn more about the direct applications to grading, instructional improvement, learning acceleration and equity on Testing 1, 2, 3: Classroom Assessment page. 

Graduation Requirements

Districts are required to teach all state standards to all students. Each academic standard is met through its associated benchmarks, which define the required knowledge and skills. Schools must provide instruction in all benchmarks, and students must demonstrate achievement of each benchmark to satisfactorily complete an academic standard. At the secondary level, students must also meet applicable credit requirements in addition to achieving the standards. Please visit the Graduation Requirement webpage for more information.

Standards Review and Revision (Rulemaking)

The Minnesota Department of Education engages in rulemaking when we are given legislative authority to do so, such as the creation, review and revision of the Minnesota K-12 Academic Standards. Please visit MDE’s Active Rulemaking page for more information.

The standards review and revision process is guided by an expert committee of Minnesotans, in accordance with Minn. Stat. 120B.021, subd. 2. To learn more about being on the committee and the impact of this work, we invite you to watch the following video testimonials from past participants:

  • Dr. Kristin Ford (post-secondary and industry perspective)
  • Kate LePage (professional and rural perspective)
  • Andrea Michaels (teacher perspective)

The following content areas are starting, in, or recently completed the rulemaking process:

Physical Education: Will begin during the 2026-27 School Year

In accordance with Minnesota Statutes, Minnesota will begin the process of reviewing and revising the statewide Minnesota K-12 Academic Standards in Physical Education (PE) beginning in the 2026-27 school year. To be notified via email of the committee application, public comment periods, and other related PE standards review and revision updates please sign up for the Physical Education Standards Review and Revision Mailing List.

Health: Started in the 2024-25 school year

In accordance with Minnesota Statutes, Minnesota is in the process of creating statewide Minnesota K-12 Academic Standards in Health. The standards are being created for Minnesota public schools, by Minnesotans. The Minnesota K-12 Academic Standards for Health creation is being conducted by a committee that includes members with varying perspectives and backgrounds across Minnesota. You can learn more about the standards review process on the MDE Academic Standards for Health Education webpage. 

As part of the Health Standards Process, the Statement of Need and Reasonableness (SONAR) was released on December 15, 2025. The SONAR describes the standards review committee’s process of writing the proposed standards and why each Anchor Standard (also known as rules) is needed and reasonable.

There was a public hearing on April 13 and 14, 2026. Following the hearing, 40585 Department of Education Post-Hearing Comment Period began and will end at 4:30 p.m. on May 4, 2026. The rebuttal comment period is May 5, 2026, through 4:30 p.m. on May 11, 2026.

Mathematics: Standards Adoption Complete

On February 25, 2025, Office of Administrative Hearing Judge approved the K-12 Academic Standards in Mathematics.  The Commissioner signed a Notice of Adoption on February 28, 2025, and the rules were published on April 7, 2025. 

  • 2022 K-12 Mathematics Standards Final Version

Social Studies: Standards Adoption Complete

In 2024, Office of Administrative Hearing Judge approved the K-12 Academic Standards in Social Studies.  These standards are scheduled for full implementation in the 2026-27 school year.

  • 2021 K-12 Academic Standards in Social Studies

Mapping the Journey from Standards to Lessons Modules

The modules available below are designed to guide educators, both individuals and teams, through a process for implementing a set of standards, from understanding the standards to examining student work. A module may include a protocol, discussion guide, video classroom demonstration or a link to resources. Each module is self-paced and can be completed in 30 to 45 minutes.

  • Webinar: The Active Relationship Between Educators, Standards, and Curriculum in Standards-Based Education - 5/27/22
